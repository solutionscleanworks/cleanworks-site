Pricing includes standard office cleaning: trash removal, vacuuming, mopping, surface disinfection, restroom sanitization, and kitchen/breakroom cleaning. Excludes specialty services like floor stripping, window washing, or post-construction.

What Actually Drives Price

1. Frequency. Daily service costs less per visit than weekly — your crew gets efficient. Weekly service costs roughly 25–40% more per visit than daily.

2. Square footage. This is the obvious one, but layout matters. A 5,000 sq ft open-plan office cleans faster than a 5,000 sq ft dental clinic with 12 treatment rooms.

3. Facility type. Medical and dental facilities require higher sanitization standards. Schools need deep cleaning during breaks. Law firms need confidential, detail-oriented service.

4. Special requirements. Floor stripping and waxing, carpet extraction, high-dusting, window cleaning — these are add-ons that get quoted separately.

5. Contract structure. Most Phoenix cleaners use one of two models:

  • Flat-rate contract: Fixed monthly price. You know exactly what you pay.
  • Hourly + materials: You pay for labor hours plus supplies. Can fluctuate month to month.

Red Flags When Quoting Cleaners

  • 🚩 Prices way below market — they're either cutting corners or will jack rates in month 2
  • 🚩 No written contract — verbal agreements favor them, not you
  • 🚩 Different crew every visit — you're re-training strangers on your facility
  • 🚩 Won't give references in your industry — they probably don't have any

Phoenix-Specific Factors

Dust. It's Phoenix — dust control is year-round, not seasonal. Any cleaner you hire should include regular high-dusting in their base service.

Monsoon season (June–September). Tracked-in mud, humidity-related issues, and more frequent floor maintenance. Ask how they handle it.

AC systems. Phoenix buildings run AC hard. Clean vents and returns mean better air quality and lower energy bills. Some cleaners include vent wiping; most don't.
