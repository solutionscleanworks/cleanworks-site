How to Choose a Commercial Cleaning Company in Phoenix — 10 Questions to Ask

Published May 18, 2026 — CleanWorks Solutions

There are hundreds of cleaning companies in Phoenix. Most of them will say the same things: "reliable," "professional," "affordable." Here are the actual questions that separate the pros from the pretenders.

1. "Do I get the same crew every visit?"
If the answer is no, you're re-training strangers on your facility every week. Consistency is the single biggest predictor of quality.
2. "Is your pricing flat-rate or hourly?"
Flat-rate means you know exactly what you pay. Hourly means you're guessing. Ask for a fixed monthly contract with clear scope.
3. "What happens if something gets missed?"
A real answer: "We come back and fix it, no charge, no argument." A bad answer: silence, deflection, or "that never happens."
4. "Can you show me your certificate of insurance?"
Any legitimate cleaner has general liability and workers' comp insurance. If they hesitate, walk away.
5. "Do you have clients in my industry?"
Cleaning a law firm is different from cleaning a daycare. Industry experience matters. Ask for references.
6. "Who supplies the cleaning products and equipment?"
Some cleaners bring their own supplies (included in price). Others expect you to provide everything. Clarify this upfront.
7. "What does your contract actually say about price increases?"
Some contracts let them raise prices with 30 days notice. Others lock rates for 12 months. Read the fine print.
8. "How do you handle security and access?"
Will crew members have keys? Alarm codes? Are they background-checked? If you have sensitive documents or equipment, this is non-negotiable.
9. "What's your cancellation policy?"
If it takes 90 days to cancel, that's a red flag. Reasonable: 30 days notice. Great: no lock-in contract at all.
10. "Can I do a walkthrough before signing anything?"
If they won't walk your facility and give you a real quote, they're probably a broker selling your contract to the lowest bidder — not an actual cleaning company.

Phoenix Bonus: The "Dust Factor"

Ask specifically how they handle dust. Phoenix buildings accumulate dust faster than most cities. A good answer includes regular high-dusting of vents, ledges, and hard-to-reach surfaces. A bad answer: "we vacuum."
